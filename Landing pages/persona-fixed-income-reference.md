# Persona Reference: Fixed Income

Source landing page: `Retrofit_CVP_Landing_FixedIncome.html`

Use this file to guide landing pages, ads, email, sales scripts, offer pages, onboarding, objection handling, and retention messaging for the Fixed Income persona.

## AI Usage Contract

- Treat this persona as budget-protective, trust-sensitive, and potentially older.
- Lead with dignity, predictability, and no-pressure clarity.
- Explain Reposit as a way to make energy costs fit a fixed income.
- Avoid patronising language, exaggerated fear, or complicated financial framing.
- Every journey step should answer: "Can I trust this, and will it fit my budget?"

## Brand Frame

Primary promise: You and Reposit: Better together.

Persona-specific interpretation: Reposit turns an existing battery into predictable monthly energy costs that support a fixed household budget.

Tone:

- Clear, respectful, patient.
- Low-pressure and practical.
- Avoid cleverness that could feel flippant about financial stress.
- Use short sentences and plain explanations.

Visual direction:

- Before/after contrast: winter bill stress vs same monthly amount.
- Use simple monthly fee tiles, budget calendars, and step-by-step process visuals.
- Palette from current page: trustworthy blue with restrained warm warning colors.
- Use readable type, high contrast, and uncluttered layouts.

## Persona Snapshot

Working name: The Budget Protector.

Likely profile:

- Retiree, pensioner, or household living on a fixed or constrained income.
- Owns a battery but may not be deeply technical.
- Feels bill spikes acutely because income does not rise with energy costs.
- Values certainty over maximising upside.
- Needs confidence that there is no sales pressure or hidden complexity.

Core belief:

- "My income is fixed. My essential costs should be predictable."

Core anxiety:

- "A large winter or quarterly bill could force trade-offs elsewhere."

Desired outcome:

- One predictable energy fee, clear terms, no surprise bills, and a sense of control.

## Buying Psychology

Dominant motivation: Budget certainty.

Decision triggers:

- Winter bills rising while pension or fixed income stays the same.
- Electricity taking a noticeable share of income during peak months.
- Government rebates ending or retailer increases arriving.
- "Same in summer. Same in winter. Every month."

Emotional drivers:

- Relief from bill shock.
- Dignity and independence.
- Desire to avoid choosing between essentials.
- Trust in a simple process with no pressure.

Risk perception:

- High sensitivity to hidden fees, lock-ins, or vague promises.
- May be cautious about one-time codes, app access, and online forms.
- May need more reassurance and plain-language support than other personas.
- Could abandon if the experience feels rushed or overly technical.

Proof requirements:

- Clear monthly amount.
- What is included and what is not.
- Simple explanation of why the offer is personalised.
- "No commitment, no pressure."
- Clear next steps after confirmation.

## Messaging Hierarchy

Primary headline territory:

- A fixed income deserves a fixed energy bill.
- Your income is fixed. Your energy bill should be too.
- No more surprise bills.
- See your fixed monthly fee.

Primary benefits:

- Surprise bills: gone.
- One fixed fee: same in summer, same in winter.
- Annual saving: express cautiously and make the household meaning tangible.

Supporting proof points:

- Fixed-income households need fixed costs.
- Reposit reads usage to size a personalised offer.
- Reposit trades the battery and passes value back through a predictable monthly number.
- The offer is held for 24 hours and there is no commitment until choosing.

CTA language:

- Lock In My Fixed Bill.
- See What I'd Pay Each Month.
- Confirm - lock in my fixed monthly bill.

Avoid:

- Aggressive urgency.
- Terms like arbitrage, VPP dispatch, wholesale spread, or demand response without explanation.
- Copy that implies poverty, helplessness, or embarrassment.
- "Earn from your battery" as the lead message.

## Journey Guidance

Awareness:

- Start with the mismatch between fixed income and variable bills.
- Use seasonal bill shock as the problem.
- Keep the opening promise simple: one predictable energy cost.

Consideration:

- Show the exact process before asking for action.
- Use "Four simple steps - no jargon, no fuss."
- Put trust copy close to the consent form.
- Make upload/manual bill fallback easy and non-technical.

Conversion:

- Reduce perceived pressure: free assessment, no commitment, no pressure.
- Use clear button labels; avoid playful CTAs.
- Make monthly fee the most prominent number on offer cards.

Onboarding:

- Confirm what the customer has locked in.
- Explain billing timing, support channels, and what happens if usage changes.
- Provide printable or email-friendly summaries where possible.

Retention:

- Reinforce budget certainty across seasons.
- Show "your fee stayed the same" during winter or market volatility.
- Keep service messages plain and practical.

## Objections And Responses

"I do not want to be tricked into a contract."

- State the assessment is free, the offer is shown before commitment, and the customer chooses whether to proceed.

"I am not good with technology."

- Keep steps simple: email, code, usage read, fixed-fee offer. Offer bill upload/manual entry fallback.

"What if I use more power?"

- Explain usage assumptions and review rules plainly.

"Why do you need access to my battery app?"

- Reposit uses read access to calculate the fixed fee from actual usage and battery data.

"Can I talk to someone?"

- For this persona, human support should be easy to find and should reinforce no-pressure guidance.

## Content Guardrails

Do:

- Use respectful, calm language.
- Make fixed monthly cost the hero.
- Explain every step in plain English.
- Show no-pressure reassurance near CTAs.

Do not:

- Use scare tactics about pensioners skipping heating unless carefully supported and necessary.
- Make the page feel like a high-pressure sale.
- Hide contract term, monthly fee, or installation cost.
- Depend on small text for important conditions.

## Product/Journey Requirements

- Accessibility matters: readable font sizes, strong contrast, clear form errors.
- Offer cards should avoid clutter and emphasise monthly predictability.
- Manual bill upload should support customers who cannot connect data automatically.
- Confirmation should produce a clear plan recap: installation cost, monthly fee, incentive, contract term.
- Follow-up emails should use "your fixed monthly fee" and "no more surprise bills" language.
