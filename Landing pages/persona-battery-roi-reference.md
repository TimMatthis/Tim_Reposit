# Persona Reference: Battery ROI

Source landing page: `Retrofit_CVP_Landing_BatteryROI.html`

Use this file to guide landing pages, ads, email, sales scripts, offer pages, onboarding, objection handling, and retention messaging for the Battery ROI persona.

## AI Usage Contract

- Treat this persona as financially motivated, proof-seeking, and already invested in home battery hardware.
- Lead with wasted value, underused assets, payback acceleration, and measurable returns.
- Keep Reposit positioned as the missing layer that turns an owned battery from passive storage into an earning asset.
- Avoid abstract sustainability claims as the primary hook. Environmental benefit can support the argument, but ROI is the decision frame.
- Every journey step should answer: "Will this make my battery investment work harder?"

## Brand Frame

Primary promise: You and Reposit: Better together.

Persona-specific interpretation: The customer already bought the expensive hardware. Reposit makes that asset smarter, more active, and more financially useful.

Tone:

- Direct, confident, numbers-led.
- Slightly provocative about idle battery capacity.
- Avoid soft lifestyle copy. This customer responds to utility, economics, and proof.
- Use plain Australian energy language: bill, battery, trading, wholesale market, fixed fee, payback.

Visual direction:

- Contrast idle vs active: "your battery right now" against "your battery with Reposit".
- Use charts, before/after comparisons, utilisation meters, and earnings/payback visuals.
- Palette from current page: amber/gold for value capture, cyan/blue for energy technology, dark background for market seriousness.
- Visuals should feel like asset performance, not green lifestyle.

## Persona Snapshot

Working name: The Battery Investor.

Likely profile:

- Homeowner with an existing battery system, commonly Solax or Sigenergy.
- Has spent significant money on solar and battery hardware.
- Believes in energy independence but is frustrated by slow payback.
- May have researched VPPs, feed-in tariffs, wholesale trading, or battery arbitrage.
- Can understand a moderately technical explanation if it is tied to money.

Core belief:

- "I paid a lot for this battery. It should do more."

Core anxiety:

- "Maybe the battery was not worth it, or I am missing value that other people are capturing."

Desired outcome:

- Faster payback, lower effective electricity cost, and confidence that the battery is being used fully.

## Buying Psychology

Dominant motivation: Return on investment.

Decision triggers:

- Underutilisation: "Average home battery uses only 30% of capacity for self-consumption."
- Missed market value: wholesale price spikes, falling feed-in tariffs, grid stability payments.
- Payback pain: battery payback without VPP is framed as 8-12 years and climbing.
- Asset optimisation: same hardware, better software, more value.

Emotional drivers:

- Frustration at idle capacity.
- Regret avoidance after a large purchase.
- Status from being a smart energy owner, not just a solar adopter.
- Desire to feel financially competent and ahead of the market.

Risk perception:

- Worries about losing control of the battery.
- Needs confidence that savings/earnings claims are credible.
- May distrust generic "earn more" promises.
- May question whether trading affects battery life, backup reserve, warranty, or comfort.

Proof requirements:

- Specific before/after economics.
- Current annual spend and proposed fixed fee.
- Explanation of how the value is created.
- Personalised offer based on actual usage and battery capacity.
- Clear disclaimers that trading revenue varies with market conditions.

## Messaging Hierarchy

Primary headline territory:

- Your battery cost a fortune. It should be earning one.
- Put your battery to work.
- Same hardware. More value.
- Your bill swings. Your battery could flatten them.

Primary benefits:

- 100% battery utilisation: trading 24/7, not just when the household is home.
- One fixed fee: set below current average spend.
- Payback accelerated: battery finally earns its keep.

Supporting proof points:

- VPP-enrolled batteries earn more than self-consumption alone.
- Feed-in tariffs are falling, making simple export less compelling.
- Wholesale price spikes create opportunities idle batteries miss.
- Personalised offer is calculated from actual usage data and battery capacity.

CTA language:

- Put My Battery to Work.
- See what my battery could earn.
- Confirm - put my battery to work.

Avoid:

- "Save the planet" as the main sell.
- "No effort" as the main sell.
- Overpromising exact earnings before the profile has been assessed.
- Dense market jargon without an immediate financial translation.

## Journey Guidance

Awareness:

- Start with wasted battery value and slow payback.
- Use the customer's sunk cost as the opening tension, but do not shame them.
- Make the market opportunity concrete: wholesale spikes, feed-in tariff decline, grid payments.

Consideration:

- Explain the mechanism: Reposit reads usage, calculates offers, trades the battery, shares value back through a fixed fee.
- Show ranked plans and how they compare against current annual spend.
- Surface control, backup, warranty, and battery-health reassurance early.

Conversion:

- Keep the form short and economically purposeful.
- "60 seconds to connect. Personalised offer in about 15 minutes. No commitment until you choose."
- The 24-hour held offer works well because this customer understands market-based pricing.

Onboarding:

- Reinforce that the battery has been upgraded from idle storage to active market participant.
- Give a simple performance dashboard after sign-up: value captured, bill stability, dispatch events, battery reserve.

Retention:

- Report value against baseline: avoided cost, fixed-fee stability, payback progress.
- Explain market events in plain terms: "Your battery responded during a price spike."

## Objections And Responses

"Will this wear out my battery?"

- Explain operating safeguards, battery limits, and reserve settings. Tie answer to long-term value, not just usage.

"What if the market changes?"

- The customer receives a fixed fee. Reposit manages market complexity behind the scenes.

"How much will I really earn?"

- Do not give a generic number. Ask for consent to read usage and build the personalised offer.

"Can I keep backup power?"

- Confirm backup/reserve settings are respected where applicable. Make control visible in onboarding.

"Is this another VPP that takes control?"

- Position Reposit as optimisation and value sharing, not a vague third-party takeover.

## Content Guardrails

Do:

- Make value leakage visible.
- Use "same hardware" and "put it to work" language.
- Show concrete financial comparison.
- Keep disclaimers clear and close to claims.

Do not:

- Make unsupported earnings guarantees.
- Hide the data consent step.
- Use guilt-based environmental copy.
- Lead with complex tariffs before the customer sees the ROI frame.

## Product/Journey Requirements

- Consent flow must feel like unlocking a valuation, not filling a form.
- Missing usage data fallback should be explicit: upload bill or enter average monthly spend.
- Offer cards should rank options by value, fixed fee, and fit.
- Confirmation should recap installation cost, monthly fee, incentive, and term.
- Follow-up emails should preserve the ROI frame: "Your battery's earning potential is ready."
