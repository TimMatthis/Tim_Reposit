# Persona 4: Green Impact

> "My battery should power more than my home."

The climate-motivated buyer. Bought solar to do the right thing, bought a battery to extend the same gesture, and is now looking for the next leverage point. The **only persona where mission language outperforms price language** — but only inside very specific guardrails.

---

## 1. Snapshot

A homeowner aged **30–60**, often a household with school-aged children, frequently in regional or outer-urban locations across NSW/VIC/SA/QLD where rooftop solar penetration is already high. Often has visible signals of climate orientation: an EV in the driveway, a heat-pump hot water system, a kitchen garden, a ScienceForChange or similar bumper sticker. They subscribe to *Renew* magazine, follow @ClimateCouncilOz, and treat the household energy system as a **lifestyle expression** rather than an appliance. They were the first in their street to install solar in 2014. They want every kWh they generate to displace as much fossil generation as physically possible.

---

## 2. Core driver

**Systemic agency.** The feeling that their individual action is *connected to a larger movement* and is materially shifting outcomes beyond their own bill. They are not motivated by saving money for its own sake — they are motivated by **proof that their effort scales** (every peak you absorb is a gas turbine that stays off). Money sits as a "pleasant side effect", not the headline.

---

## 3. Inner monologue

- "Self-consumption is fine, but what's it actually doing for the grid?"
- "Every time I see a gas peaker fire up I think *that should be a battery somewhere*."
- "I bought solar in 2014. I'm done playing small."
- "I'd take a slightly worse financial outcome if it actually moved the dial."
- "Show me the carbon avoided, not just the dollars saved."
- "I want to be part of *something*, not just a customer."

---

## 4. Tone principles

1. **Frame as collective.** This is the **one persona where "fleet", "join", and "together" land hard**. The other five reject those words. Don't repurpose this voice for them.
2. **Lead with impact, not bills.** The financial outcome is the second sentence, not the first. Inverting that order causes drop-off.
3. **Be specific about the system.** "Gas peakers stay off" beats "cleaner grid". Name the mechanism — peak shaving, demand response, grid services — but in human English.
4. **Avoid greenwash.** This persona has been burnt by greenwashing more than any other. Vague claims ("better for the planet") read as suspicious; specific claims ("displaces ~X tonnes of CO₂/yr in your fleet") read as credible.
5. **Hold the dual payoff together.** *"Save money. Clean the grid. Same decision."* The whole proposition is that they don't have to choose.

---

## 5. Vocabulary

### Use
- "Fleet of 2,000+ batteries"
- "Trading collectively"
- "Gas peakers stay off"
- "Peak demand absorbed"
- "Renewable transition"
- "Systemic impact"
- "Your battery joins others making the grid cleaner"
- "Together" / "fleet" / "join" *(unique to this persona)*
- "Measurable difference"

### Avoid
- "Eco-friendly" *(generic, low-credibility)*
- "Going green" *(2010s register)*
- "Save the planet" *(too grand, ironically un-motivating)*
- "Net zero" *(politicised; many readers parse it as marketing)*
- "Carbon offsets" *(this persona may actively distrust them)*
- "Disrupt the energy industry" *(rebellion grammar; doesn't fit their cooperative driver)*

---

## 6. Hooks that work

1. **The fleet-multiplier hook.** *"One battery helps your home. Thousands change the grid."* This is the single highest-converting line for this persona — it directly names the leverage they crave.
2. **The displaced-peaker hook.** *"Every peak your battery absorbs is a gas turbine that stays off."* Concrete causal chain, no vagueness.
3. **The dual-payoff hook.** *"You save money. The grid gets cleaner. Same action."* — frames Reposit as not making them choose between conscience and budget.
4. **The "next step for solar owners" hook.** Acknowledges they were early to solar; positions Reposit as their next, more impactful action — not a starter pack.
5. **The ambient impact band.** Show real-time or near-real-time fleet metrics: "MWh dispatched today", "peakers avoided this month", "households contributing".

---

## 7. Trust requirements

Before this persona will share their data, they need to believe:

- The **fleet is real**. 2,000+ households with verifiable sources (Reposit's own homepage and case studies). They will Google.
- The **impact claim is auditable**. If you say "displaces gas peakers", be ready to point to AEMO dispatch data or a Reposit-published methodology.
- The **business model isn't extractive**. They need to see that the revenue Reposit earns is plowed back into expanding the fleet / lowering prices, not just shareholder return.
- The product is **Australian-made and Australian-owned** (Reposit is, and it matters disproportionately to this segment).
- The **carbon framing is honest** — i.e. "we displace gas peakers in your specific NEM region" rather than a generic "saves X tonnes of CO₂/year" that reads as marketing math.

---

## 8. Anti-patterns

- **Bill-first messaging.** Leading with "Save $X/year" demotes the impact and signals you've misread them.
- **Aggressive urgency.** "Only 50 spots in the fleet!" reads as artificial scarcity; this persona prefers a calm, perpetually-open invitation.
- **Lone-hero framing.** "*You* will save the planet" loses them. They want to be part of something larger, not the protagonist.
- **Unaudited claims.** "100% green energy" without a methodology link is greenwashing. Either link it or drop it.
- **Tribal exclusion.** Avoid "the smart ones" or "join the elite" — this persona is *small-d* democratic in their environmentalism.

## 10. Hero formula

```
PANE A     : The grid right now   (chaos pane — gas peakers
             firing during evening peak, coal still ~50% of mix)
PANE B     : The grid, with your battery in it   (calm pane —
             coordinated battery fleet absorbing the peak,
             gas turbines staying off)
H1         : Your battery can power
             more than just your home.
SUB        : Mission sentence ("You bought solar to make a
             difference. A battery to store it."). Mechanism
             sentence ("Reposit connects it to a fleet of
             2,000+ batteries that trade collectively"). Dual
             payoff sentence ("You get a fixed monthly fee.
             The grid gets cleaner.").
TAGLINE    : "You and Reposit: Better together — for the grid,
             for the planet"
```

The chaos pane *must* show the systemic problem (the grid as it is) — not the buyer's own dashboard. Every other persona uses chaos to mirror the buyer's frustration; for Green Impact, chaos mirrors *the world's* frustration. That inversion is the whole psychology.

## 11. CTA verbs

This is the **one persona where movement-grammar is appropriate**. Use it.

- ✅ **"Join the Grid Revolution"** *(canonical — would be off-brand for any other persona)*
- ✅ **"Add My Battery to the Fleet"**
- ✅ **"Make My Battery Count"**
- ❌ "Get Started" *(generic; wasted)*
- ❌ "Save Money" *(off-driver)*
- ❌ "Activate My Account" *(transactional, kills the mission framing)*

The button colour stays Reposit green — the verb does all the persona-specific lifting.

## 14. Conversion psychology summary

The Green Impact buyer is converting on **leverage, not on virtue**. Their identity is already settled — they don't need you to remind them they care about the planet, and any attempt to do so reads as flattery. What they want is **proof that their next action moves the dial more than their last one did**. Your job in every artifact is to extend their existing solar/battery decision into a more systemic frame: from "my home is cleaner" to "my home is part of a fleet that's measurably shifting the grid". Use collective grammar (fleet, together, join) — but only here, and only when paired with concrete, auditable claims about gas peakers, peak demand, MWh dispatched. Pair the impact promise with the financial one in the same breath, never sequentially, because the whole point is that this persona shouldn't have to choose. The CTA is movement-shaped because the conversion is movement-shaped: they aren't buying a product, they're escalating a commitment they already have.

## 15. Email rules: pre-offer constraint

The **offer-ready email is sent before the offer is calculated** (immediately after the Solax/Sigenstor OAuth completes). At email-send time we do not yet know this user's annual saving, fixed monthly fee, personal carbon impact, or offer-expiry timestamp. The **only personalised element in the email body** is the deep link to *their* offer page (`{{ contact.retro_offer }}`).

For full token rules see [`README.md` → Email rule: nothing personalised except the URL](README.md#email-rule-nothing-personalised-except-the-url).

This persona is the most likely to forgive a generic email *if* the dual-payoff (saving + impact) is preserved. Don't pick one over the other.

### What this persona's email body must contain

| Element | Generic-true copy | Why it works for Green Impact |
|---|---|---|
| **Hero — fleet tile** | *Your fleet* / **2,000+ batteries** / *Trading collectively. Gas peakers stay off.* | Fleet size is generic-true (every recipient joins the same fleet) and is the dial-mover this persona came for. Keep aligned with the live landing page; never round down. |
| **Hero — saving tile** | *Your saving* / **Fixed monthly fee** / *Below your current variable plan.* | The dual-payoff stays paired with the fleet tile in a single row. Same breath, never sequential. |
| **Validity** | *"Your offer + your fleet contribution are calculated together. Both are locked for **24 hours** from when your offer is generated. After that we re-run against the latest market data."* | This persona accepts logistic urgency when it's framed as "your impact is being modelled with your saving". |
| **Footer date** | *"...because you recently connected your battery to Reposit."* | No specific timestamp. Anonymous-safe. |
| **CTA** | *"Add My Battery to the Fleet →"* on a single green button → `{{ contact.retro_offer }}`. | Movement-shaped verb. Matches the buyer's escalating-commitment psychology. |
| **Optional clarion line** | *"Save money. Clean the grid. Same decision."* | Already on the landing page. The **only** persona for which a quiet-clarion line in the email is on-strategy — but only this exact phrasing, no "revolution" upgrade. |

### Email anti-patterns specific to Green Impact

- **No personal carbon-impact figures in the email body.** Don't say "your battery displaces X tonnes of CO₂/yr". The fleet figure (2,000+) is generic-true; a *per-user* tonnage is a calculation that only the offer page can show with assumptions attached.
- **No outdated fleet figure.** If the live landing page says 2,000+, the email says 2,000+. If the live landing page is updated to 6,000+, the email follows. **Never** allow the two to drift.
- **No "revolution" / "movement" hype upgrade.** This persona accepts collective grammar (fleet, together, join) but rejects activist grammar. The line is real. Stay calm-clarion, never loud-clarion.
- **No saving-without-impact framing.** If the email body mentions the saving without mentioning the fleet contribution, you've lost the persona. They came for both.
- **No grid-villain copy.** "Beat the retailers" / "stick it to the grid" — wrong tribe. They want gas peakers off, not retailers humiliated.
- **No placeholder dollar figures.** Even "save up to $X" with disclaimers undercuts the purity of "fixed monthly fee, below your current variable plan".

### Tease-the-click lines that work for this persona

These are the only acceptable ways to refer to the personalised numbers *without* showing them:

- "Your fleet contribution + your saving are inside."
- "Tap to see how much your battery shifts the dial."
- "Personalised impact + personalised saving, calculated together."
- "Save money. Clean the grid. Same decision. One tap away."

The actual calculated figures — your modelled annual saving, your battery's projected MWh dispatched into peak periods, your fleet share — live on the offer page the user clicks through to. **The email is the door, not the room.**
