# Persona 1: Bill Fairness *(master / default)*

> "I have a battery. I shouldn't still have a bill."

This is the **default Reposit retrofit buyer** — the one we assume when we don't yet know which segment a contact belongs to. Every other persona guide in this folder exists to amplify, never to replace, the principles laid out here.

---

## 1. Snapshot

A homeowner aged 35–65 who has spent five-figures on a solar + battery system within the last 1–6 years. They expected the system to *eliminate* their electricity bill. Instead they still receive a quarterly statement that looks suspiciously similar to what their less-equipped neighbours pay. They are mildly outraged, but not yet activist about it — they assume the issue is technical (tariffs, time-of-use, daily supply charges) rather than that they were oversold. They are open to a single, simple solution that finally closes the loop.

---

## 2. Core driver

**Fairness.** Specifically: a quiet sense of injustice that the system they paid for is not delivering on its implied promise. Not anger — disappointment with capacity for action. They want to be told, plainly, that there's a way to fix it.

---

## 3. Inner monologue

- "I paid $15k for this thing. Why am I still getting a bill at all?"
- "The supply charge alone is $1.20 a day before I use a single watt."
- "My neighbour without solar pays about the same as me. That can't be right."
- "If I have to install another piece of equipment I'll lose my mind."
- "I don't want to fight my retailer. I just want this to be over."

---

## 4. Tone principles

1. **Direct.** State the asymmetry plainly. Don't hedge, don't soften.
2. **Calm conviction.** No exclamation marks, no "amazing news!". This buyer wants a steady hand.
3. **Outcome-first.** Lead with what changes ($0 bill, one fixed fee), not how Reposit works.
4. **Australian, not American.** "Brilliant", "sorted", "no fuss" are fine. "Awesome", "you guys", "rockstar" are not.
5. **Evidence over adjectives.** Every claim should have a number, a duration, or a guarantee attached.

---

## 5. Vocabulary

### Use
- "You already have a battery"
- "You shouldn't still have a bill"
- "Daily supply charges, gone"
- "One fixed monthly fee"
- "Set lower than what you pay today"
- "60 seconds to connect"
- "Personalised offer in ~15 minutes"
- "No commitment until you choose"

### Avoid
- "Revolutionary" / "game-changing" / "disruptive"
- "Smart energy revolution" *(too activist for this persona)*
- "Unlock" / "reveal" / "secret" *(clickbait register)*
- "You qualify!" *(retailer / scam grammar)*
- Exclamation marks anywhere in body copy
- "Save up to" — replace with the actual indicative figure plus a transparent disclaimer

---

## 6. Hooks that work

1. **The asymmetry hook.** *"You already have a battery. You should not still have a bill."* — name the unfairness, then name the fix.
2. **The supply-charge hook.** Most readers don't realise daily supply is the largest fixed cost on their bill. Showing $0 supply included is a sharp wake-up.
3. **The 15-minute hook.** "From consent to offer in ~15 minutes" beats every competitor's "we'll be in touch within 5 business days" by a wide margin.
4. **The "your existing setup stays" hook.** Removes the largest objection (more disruption / more capex) before it forms.
5. **The compare-card hook.** Side-by-side "Variable bill (today)" vs "Fixed fee (with Reposit)" with the same household profile.

---

## 7. Trust requirements

Before this persona will click, they need to believe:

- This is **not a retailer switch**. Reposit is something different — they keep the equipment they already trust.
- There is **no commitment up front**. Free assessment, no payment today, walk away if the offer isn't right.
- The **fixed fee is genuinely lower** than their current variable spend (not "could be" — *is*, with a personalised number).
- **Reposit knows what it's doing**. 10+ years, 6,000+ Australian homes (verified). Australian-owned and built.

---

## 8. Anti-patterns

- **Mystery-box CTAs.** "Reveal", "behind the doors", blurred amounts — this persona reads them as bait and bounces.
- **Tribe rhetoric.** "Join the revolution" / "movement" — they don't want a movement, they want a fixed bill.
- **Multiple CTAs.** A single green button beats two of equal weight every time.
- **Talking down to them.** This buyer is technical enough to have specced their original system. Don't explain what a kWh is.
- **Ambiguous validity windows.** "Limited time only" reads as fake urgency. "Held for 24 hours" reads as logistic reality. Use the latter.

---

## 9. Subject + preheader patterns

| Subject | Preheader | Why it works |
|---|---|---|
| "Your Reposit retrofit offer is ready" | "60 seconds to connect, fixed fee in ~15 minutes." | Plain, transactional, sets expectations. |
| "$0 bill: your personalised numbers" | "Daily supply included. Fixed fee set below your current spend." | Names the prize and the proof. |
| "Your battery, your offer, locked for 24 hours" | "Your personalised number is inside. No commitment until you choose." | Concrete urgency, no loaded language. |

Avoid: ALL-CAPS, multiple emojis, "🔥 Final reminder", "Don't miss this".

## 10. Hero formula

```
EYEBROW    : Reposit No Bill Retrofit
H1         : You Already Have a Battery.
             You Should Not Still Have a Bill.
SUB        : One sentence stating the mechanism (smart trading +
             retrofit), one sentence stating the outcome ($0 bill +
             one fixed fee), one optional clause stating the timing
             (~15 minutes to your offer).
```

The H1 is two short clauses. Period in the middle. Caps optional but consistent. Each clause does one job: name the asset, name the absurdity.

---

## 11. CTA verbs

The CTA should sound like the buyer's own next thought, not a marketing imperative.

- ✅ **"Get My Free Assessment"** *(canonical for this persona)*
- ✅ **"See What I'd Pay"**
- ✅ **"Show Me My Number"**
- ❌ "Claim My Offer" *(too retailer)*
- ❌ "Unlock My Saving" *(too clickbait)*
- ❌ "Join Now" *(too tribal)*

Match the button colour token (`#2de49d → #15b876`), 11 px radius, weight 800. Single CTA, never two of equal weight on the same screen.

---

## 12. Trust chips (3–4)

For this persona, the chip row should reduce the *three biggest unspoken objections*:

| Chip | Reduces objection |
|---|---|
| **$0 Electricity Bill** | "What's the actual outcome?" |
| **One Fixed Monthly Fee** | "What does that cost?" |
| **Your Existing Battery Stays** | "Do I need to buy more hardware?" |
| **We Handle the Setup** | "How much hassle is this?" |

Use four if you have the room, three if not. Drop "Cancel any time" for this persona — they're not anxious about lock-in yet, so the chip wastes a slot. Save it for Persona 5 (Energy Uncertainty).

## 14. Conversion psychology summary

The Bill Fairness buyer is not won by enthusiasm. They are won by **the relief of being heard**. Your job in every artifact is to mirror their unspoken complaint back at them — *"you have a battery and you shouldn't still have a bill"* — and then immediately offer the simplest possible exit from that complaint. No hype, no movement, no game. State the asymmetry, state the fix, state the cost (in time, in dollars, in commitment), and get out of the way. The CTA is not "do this exciting thing"; it is "get the calculation that ends the irritation". Calm conviction is the highest-converting register for this persona, and every other persona in this folder exists as a more amplified variant of it for buyers who happen to be more anxious, more analytical, more environmental, or more time-poor than the default.

## 15. Email rules: pre-offer constraint

The **offer-ready email is sent before the offer is calculated** (immediately after the Solax/Sigenstor OAuth completes). At email-send time we do not yet know this user's annual saving, fixed monthly fee, or offer-expiry timestamp. The **only personalised element in the email body** is the deep link to *their* offer page (`{{ contact.retro_offer }}`).

For full token rules see [`README.md` → Email rule: nothing personalised except the URL](README.md#email-rule-nothing-personalised-except-the-url).

### What this persona's email body must contain

| Element | Generic-true copy | Why it works for Bill Fairness |
|---|---|---|
| **Hero tile** | *Your electricity bill* / **$0** / *Replaced by one fixed monthly fee, set below your current variable spend. Your personalised number is inside.* | "$0 bill" is the No Bill™ brand promise — true for every retrofit customer, and the exact prize this persona came for. |
| **Validity** | *"Locked for **24 hours** from when your offer is generated."* | Logistic reality, not fake urgency. This persona reads "limited time only" as a retailer move and bounces. |
| **Footer date** | *"...because you recently connected your battery to Reposit."* | Friendly and generic. No first-name greeting (Reposit is anonymous-safe). |
| **CTA** | A single green button → `{{ contact.retro_offer }}`. | Single CTA always beats two of equal weight for this persona. |

### Email anti-patterns specific to Bill Fairness

- **No placeholder dollar figures.** "Estimated saving: $___" or "Save up to $X" reads as retailer dishonesty. The number must appear *only* on the offer page, and *only* as a real calculated value.
- **No countdown timers or "expires soon" language** in the body. The 24-hour hold is logistical, not promotional. State it once, calmly, in the validity panel.
- **No multi-CTA layouts.** A "View My Offer" primary plus a "Learn more" secondary halves conversion for this persona. One green button. That's it.
- **No "Your offer has been unlocked!"** energy. The email is a notification, not a reveal.

### Tease-the-click lines that work for this persona

These are the only acceptable ways to refer to the personalised numbers *without* showing them:

- "Your personalised number is inside."
- "Your personalised number is inside, calculated from your last 12 months of usage."
- "Tap to see what you'd actually pay."
- "Your offer is ready. ~15 minutes of work, calmly modelled, one tap away."

The actual calculated figures live on the offer page the user clicks through to. **The email is the door, not the room.**
