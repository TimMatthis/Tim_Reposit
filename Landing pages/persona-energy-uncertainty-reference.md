# Persona Reference: Energy Uncertainty

Source landing page: `Retrofit_CVP_Landing_EnergyUncertainty.html`

Use this file to guide landing pages, ads, email, sales scripts, offer pages, onboarding, objection handling, and retention messaging for the Energy Uncertainty persona.

## AI Usage Contract

- Treat this persona as volatility-averse and emotionally tired of unpredictable bills.
- Lead with control, predictability, and insulation from market chaos.
- Explain Reposit as the buffer between the household and energy market volatility.
- Avoid making the customer feel financially unsophisticated. They are seeking calm, not a lecture.
- Every journey step should answer: "Will this stop energy surprises?"
- Do not use em dashes in generated copy. Use commas, full stops, colons, or shorter sentences instead. Avoid obvious AI tells such as formulaic transitions, inflated claims, repetitive three-part phrasing, and generic phrases like "unlock the power of" or "take control of your energy future."

## Brand Frame

Primary promise: You and Reposit: Better together.

Persona-specific interpretation: The market can keep changing, but the customer does not have to absorb every shock.

Tone:

- Calm, reassuring, decisive.
- Show empathy for uncertainty without becoming alarmist.
- Use contrast: market chaos vs household control.
- Keep the product explanation simple and confidence-building.

Visual direction:

- Before/after contrast: fear opening the bill vs not caring what the market did.
- Use "spiky line to flat line" visuals, fixed monthly fee tiles, and bill predictability.
- Palette from current page: warm volatility colors balanced by green/blue stability.
- Avoid overly technical trading visuals upfront.

## Persona Snapshot

Working name: The Volatility Avoider.

Likely profile:

- Homeowner with an existing battery.
- Feels squeezed by electricity prices, cost of living, fuel volatility, and news about price rises.
- Wants to reduce exposure to energy market surprises.
- May not care about battery optimisation as much as bill certainty.
- Responds to "one fixed fee below your current average" more than "trading revenue".

Core belief:

- "I cannot control the market, but I need to control my household budget."

Core anxiety:

- "The next bill could be the one I did not plan for."

Desired outcome:

- A stable, predictable energy cost that removes the emotional load of bill uncertainty.

## Buying Psychology

Dominant motivation: Control.

Decision triggers:

- Winter bills forecast to rise.
- Wholesale electricity spot price surges.
- Household budgets squeezed by rising essentials.
- Global and local instability flowing into energy prices.
- Variable retailer charges and supply fees replaced by one fixed fee.

Emotional drivers:

- Relief.
- Desire to stop bracing for bad news.
- Need for confidence when planning household expenses.
- Preference for simple certainty over potentially higher but uncertain upside.

Risk perception:

- Worries that "fixed fee" has catches.
- May fear being locked into a bad deal.
- Needs reassurance that the offer is personalised and below current average spend.
- Needs plain-language explanation of what charges are replaced.

Proof requirements:

- Current estimated annual spend.
- Proposed fixed monthly fee.
- Clear statement of what is included.
- "No commitment until you choose."
- Personalised offer held for 24 hours.

## Messaging Hierarchy

Primary headline territory:

- You cannot control the market. You can control your bill.
- Get one fixed price.
- Fine for months - then the bill you did not budget for.
- Your personalised offer in about 15 minutes.

Primary benefits:

- Variable bills: gone.
- One fixed fee: below current average, every month.
- Offer speed: about 15 minutes from consent to personalised result.

Supporting proof points:

- Retailer charges and supply fees are replaced by one fixed fee.
- Reposit trades the battery and shares the value back through the plan.
- The fixed fee is sized using the customer's actual usage profile.
- The customer does not commit until the offer is visible.

CTA language:

- Get My Fixed Price.
- See What I'd Pay.
- Confirm - lock in my fixed fee.

Avoid:

- "Earn money from your battery" as the lead.
- Market jargon that makes the solution feel complex.
- Fear-only copy without a fast path to relief.
- Savings claims without explaining the fixed-fee mechanism.

## Journey Guidance

Awareness:

- Start with the emotional pattern: energy feels fine until an unexpected bill arrives.
- Use market uncertainty as the problem, but frame Reposit as calm control.
- The hook should be bill stability, not technical optimisation.

Consideration:

- Show exactly what changes: from variable retailer bills to one fixed Reposit fee.
- Keep proof visible: current annual spend, monthly fee, usage profile basis.
- Use "below your current average" carefully and only where backed by the offer.

Conversion:

- Make the consent step feel safe and quick.
- Use reassurance: "We only read your data to size your offer."
- Keep CTAs focused on seeing or locking in the fixed fee.

Onboarding:

- Reinforce that Better together starts as budget certainty.
- Provide a simple explanation of what happens next: agreement, billing, provisioning.

Retention:

- Report the absence of surprises.
- Show "what you would have paid" vs "your fixed fee" during volatile periods.
- Translate market events into household protection: "Your fee stayed the same."

## Objections And Responses

"What is the catch?"

- Be explicit about inclusions, term, fees, and what happens after the offer expires.

"What if my usage changes?"

- Explain how the plan is based on usage profile and what review/adjustment rules apply.

"Why do you need my battery app email?"

- Reposit needs secure read access to usage and battery data to calculate the offer.

"Will I lose control of my battery?"

- Explain operational controls simply and connect them back to fixed-fee value.

"Can prices still rise for me?"

- Clarify the fixed-fee terms and any contract boundaries. Avoid broad guarantees beyond the actual product.

## Content Guardrails

Do:

- Use calm contrast: market chaos vs fixed household cost.
- Make the next step feel quick and low-risk.
- Use plain copy around charges replaced by the fixed fee.
- Keep "no commitment until you choose" prominent.

Do not:

- Overuse crisis language.
- Lead with wholesale trading mechanics.
- Bury plan terms.
- Make universal "prices will not change" claims unless the product terms support them.

## Product/Journey Requirements

- Every cost claim should be tied to the customer's profile or labelled indicative.
- Manual bill upload should feel like a normal fallback, not a failure.
- Offer cards should make the fixed monthly fee visually dominant.
- Confirmation should use "lock in my fixed fee" language.
- Follow-up emails should focus on the fixed-fee offer and its expiry.
