# Persona Reference: Set And Forget

Source landing page: `Retrofit_CVP_Landing_SetAndForget.html`

Use this file to guide landing pages, ads, email, sales scripts, offer pages, onboarding, objection handling, and retention messaging for the Set And Forget persona.

## AI Usage Contract

- Treat this persona as convenience-led and decision-fatigued.
- Lead with removing energy admin, retailer switching, tariff management, and monitoring.
- Explain Reposit as the energy autopilot: one fixed fee, zero ongoing decisions.
- Avoid detailed market mechanics unless needed for trust or objections.
- Every journey step should answer: "Can I stop thinking about electricity?"

## Brand Frame

Primary promise: You and Reposit: Better together.

Persona-specific interpretation: Reposit handles the complexity so the customer gets a simple fixed outcome.

Tone:

- Minimal, confident, slightly witty.
- Energy should feel boring.
- Avoid overexplaining on first touch; use progressive disclosure.
- Respect their time at every step.

Visual direction:

- Before/after contrast: tariff chaos and admin overload vs one simple number.
- Use simple dashboards, checklists reduced to zero, autopilot language, and calm home imagery.
- Palette from current page: teal/purple calm technology.
- Avoid dense tables and charts above the fold.

## Persona Snapshot

Working name: The Energy Delegator.

Likely profile:

- Homeowner with a battery who does not want to manage energy details.
- May have tried comparing retailers, tariffs, and feed-in rates and found it tedious.
- Values time, simplicity, and mental load reduction.
- Less interested in maximising every dollar than in a good automated outcome.
- Responds to "we handle everything" and "one fixed fee."

Core belief:

- "I have better things to do than manage electricity."

Core anxiety:

- "Energy is too complex, and I am probably missing something."

Desired outcome:

- Stop monitoring plans, tariffs, battery windows, retailer changes, and bills.

## Buying Psychology

Dominant motivation: Convenience.

Decision triggers:

- Time wasted comparing energy plans.
- Too many retailers and tariffs.
- Feed-in rates, demand tariffs, and export limits changing.
- Battery owners being asked to monitor peak usage manually.
- "Reposit customers: zero energy decisions since sign-up."

Emotional drivers:

- Relief from decision fatigue.
- Desire to delegate to a competent system.
- Preference for simplicity over perfect optimisation.
- Satisfaction from making one decision that removes many future decisions.

Risk perception:

- Worries that "we handle everything" may hide important control loss.
- Needs enough explanation to trust automation.
- May abandon if setup feels like more admin than promised.
- May be skeptical of a product that claims to make energy effortless.

Proof requirements:

- Very short setup path.
- Clear one fixed fee.
- Explanation that Reposit reads usage, builds offer, then handles trading/admin.
- Clear statement that there is no commitment until choosing.

## Messaging Hierarchy

Primary headline territory:

- You have better things to do than manage your electricity.
- Energy should be boring. Let's make it boring.
- Stop managing energy. Start ignoring it.
- You just made your last energy decision.

Primary benefits:

- Decisions required: zero.
- One fixed fee: same number every month.
- Things to monitor: nothing.

Supporting proof points:

- 30+ retailers, changing tariffs, falling feed-in rates, demand tariffs, export limits.
- Reposit handles trading, plan complexity, and fixed-fee outcome.
- Setup takes about a minute; personalised offer takes about 15 minutes.

CTA language:

- Just Fix It For Me.
- Make energy boring.
- Confirm - make energy boring.

Avoid:

- Long education before CTA.
- Financial optimisation jargon.
- Moral or environmental pressure.
- Asking for too many choices in the funnel.

## Journey Guidance

Awareness:

- Start with the admin burden: tariffs, peak rates, feed-in credits, retailer switching.
- Make the customer feel permission to stop caring.
- Position Reposit as a clean delegation decision.

Consideration:

- Keep product explanation simple: connect battery, read usage, build offer, one fixed fee, Reposit handles the rest.
- Use short sections and clear icons.
- Provide detail only where it reduces risk.

Conversion:

- The form should feel like "the only step that needs you."
- Button labels should be action-light and outcome-heavy.
- Minimise form friction and avoid optional preference questions unless truly needed.

Onboarding:

- Reinforce that setup is complete and no further decisions are needed.
- Provide a plain confirmation of what Reposit will handle.
- Avoid sending frequent operational noise unless it affects the customer.

Retention:

- Communicate only meaningful summaries.
- Reinforce time saved and stable billing.
- Avoid pushing the customer into dashboards they did not ask for.

## Objections And Responses

"What exactly do you handle?"

- Reposit handles battery trading/optimisation and converts the value into one fixed fee, based on the agreed plan.

"Will I need to keep checking anything?"

- No routine monitoring should be required. Important plan or billing updates should be surfaced clearly.

"What if I want control?"

- Explain available controls and support boundaries, but keep the default framed as delegation.

"How do I know the fixed fee is fair?"

- It is built from actual usage and battery data; the offer is shown before commitment.

"Is setup really quick?"

- The customer enters battery app email, verifies code, and Reposit calculates the offer. Manual bill upload exists if data is incomplete.

## Content Guardrails

Do:

- Keep copy short and decisive.
- Use "one fixed fee", "zero decisions", "we handle everything."
- Make friction visible only when necessary.
- Preserve the promise of low admin across the entire journey.

Do not:

- Add unnecessary configurators or preference sliders.
- Overload the customer with energy education.
- Send verbose onboarding unless required.
- Use "smart home" novelty language instead of practical convenience.

## Product/Journey Requirements

- The conversion flow should have the fewest possible steps.
- Progress indicators should reassure that the customer is nearly done.
- Offer cards should not require complex comparison.
- Confirmation should say the set-and-forget plan is ready.
- Follow-up emails should be short, action-oriented, and sparse.
